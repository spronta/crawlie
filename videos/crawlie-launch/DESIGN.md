## Style Prompt

Crawlie launch video: crisp, local-first technical product energy with the restraint of the desktop app. The frame should feel like a fast audit tool for humans and agents: precise typography, terminal-like data, clean panels, and severity colors used as signals. Dark canvas for launch contrast, with white/near-white type, blue action accents, green success/readiness, amber caution, and red errors. Motion should be confident and structured, like UI states snapping into place during a crawl.

## Colors

- Background: #080a0f
- Panel: #101319
- Text: #f5f7fb
- Muted text: #9aa3b2
- Border: #273040
- Action blue: #3b9eff
- Success green: #3ddc91
- Warning amber: #f5b544
- Error red: #ff6166

## Typography

- Primary: Geist, system sans-serif fallback
- Data/code: Geist Mono, ui-monospace fallback
- Use very large, heavy display text for launch statements; use mono for commands, metrics, JSON, and crawl traces.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use generic neon-purple SaaS gradients.
- Do not make severity colors decorative; they must map to product meaning.
- Do not use Inter, Roboto, Poppins, or other common template defaults.
- Do not use stock imagery or fake browser chrome as the main visual.
- Do not soften the interface into beige, rounded marketing cards, or ornamental blobs.
